1,659

A data sculpture about food security in Massachusetts during the height of the pandemic.

The pandemic has effected so many aspects of our lives, amplifying disparities and challenges that already existed. For far too many household simply having access to enough food to eat is a routine struggle. During the hardest time of the pandemic here, March and April of 2020, an average of 1,659 Massachusetts households applied every day for SNAP (the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program). Each of those households is represented in this data sculpture with one knife, spoon, or fork.
